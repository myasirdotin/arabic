📌 Advanced I'rab (الإعراب) - Case Endings in Detail

الإعراب — The Grammatical System of Arabic

I'rab refers to the vowel changes at the end of nouns (and verbs) that indicate their grammatical function in a sentence. Mastering this is key to Arabic fluency!

✅ The Four Cases of Arabic Nouns:

Case Arabic Ending Function Example
Nominative الرفع ـُ / ـُو Subject/Doer الوَلَدُ يَلْعَبُ (The boy plays)
Accusative النصب ـَ / ـَي Object/After particles رَأَيْتُ الوَلَدَ (I saw the boy)
Genitive الجر ـِ / ـِي After prepositions ذَهَبْتُ إِلَى الوَلَدِ (I went to the boy)
Jazm* الجزم ـْ (Sukūn) For verbs only لَمْ يَلْعَبْ (He didn't play)

*Jazm applies to verbs, not nouns.

🎯 Particles That Affect I'rab:

1. Nasb Particles (حروف النصب)

Force accusative case (ـَ):

  • إِنَّ (Verily)
  • أَنَّ (That)
  • لَنْ (Will not)
إِنَّ الطالِبَ مُجْتَهِدٌ
Verily the student (acc.) is hardworking.

2. Jarr Particles (حروف الجر)

Force genitive case (ـِ):

  • فِي (In)
  • عَلَى (On)
  • مِنْ (From)
الكِتَابُ فِي الحَقِيبَةِ
The book is in the bag (gen.).

📘 Special Cases:

1. Diptotes (ممنوع من الصرف)

Some nouns never take tanween and always end with ـَ in nasb/jarr:

أَحْمَدُ → رَأَيْتُ أَحْمَدَ (Ahmad)
مَسَاجِدُ → ذَهَبْتُ إِلَى مَسَاجِدَ (Mosques)

2. Fixed-Ending Nouns

Some nouns don't change:

  • Proper names: عُثْمَانُ (Uthman)
  • Foreign words: تِلِفْزْيُونُ (Television)

🧠 I'rab in Action:

جَاءَ مُحَمَّدٌ (Muhammad came) → مرفوع (ـٌ)

رَأَيْتُ مُحَمَّدًا (I saw Muhammad) → منصوب (ـً)

سَلَّمْتُ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ (I greeted Muhammad) → مجرور (ـٍ)
