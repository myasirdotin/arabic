📌 Arabic Pronouns (الضمائر)

المتكلم، المخاطب، الغائب — I, You, He/She/They

Arabic pronouns come in attached (suffix) and detached forms. They indicate person, gender, and number.

✅ Detached Pronouns (الضمائر المنفصلة):

English Arabic Type
I أَنَا 1st person
You (masc.) أَنْتَ 2nd person
You (fem.) أَنْتِ 2nd person
He هُوَ 3rd person
She هِيَ 3rd person
We نَحْنُ 1st plural

🎯 Attached Pronouns (الضمائر المتصلة):

English Arabic Example
My ـِي كِتابِي (my book)
Your (masc.) ـكَ بَيْتُكَ (your house)
Your (fem.) ـكِ بَيْتُكِ (your house)
His ـهُ قَلَمُهُ (his pen)
Her ـها قَلَمُها (her pen)

📘 Subject Pronouns in Sentences:

أَنَا طالِبٌ (I am a student)

هِيَ مُعَلِّمَةٌ (She is a teacher)

نَحْنُ فِي المَدْرَسَةِ (We are at school)

📝 Possessive Pronouns Chart:

Person Singular Plural
1st ـِي / ـيَ ـنَا
2nd (masc.) ـكَ ـكُمْ
2nd (fem.) ـكِ ـكُنَّ
3rd (masc.) ـهُ ـهُمْ
3rd (fem.) ـها ـهُنَّ

💡 Pro Tip:

Attached pronouns always follow the noun's case ending:

رَأَيْتُ كِتابَهُ (I saw his book - accusative)

ذَهَبْتُ إِلى بَيْتِهِ (I went to his house - genitive)

✍️ Practice:

Add the correct pronoun:

  1. This is ____ book (my) → هَذا كِتابِي
  2. ____ teacher is kind (your fem.) → مُعَلِّمَتُكِ لَطِيفَةٌ
  3. ____ house is big (their masc.) → بَيْتُهُمْ كَبِيرٌ
