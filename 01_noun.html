📌 What is a Noun in Arabic?

الاسم (Al-Ism) — The Noun

A noun in Arabic is any word that refers to:
• A person (e.g., أحمد — Ahmad)
• A place (e.g., مكة — Makkah)
• A thing (e.g., كتاب — Book)
• An idea or concept (e.g., عدالة — Justice)

In Arabic, nouns are categorized into different types based on their characteristics and functions.

✅ Features of Arabic Nouns:

Arabic nouns have four main properties: View Image

  • Gender: Masculine (مذكر) or Feminine (مؤنث)
  • Number: Singular (مفرد), Dual (مثنى), or Plural (جمع)
  • Case Endings (الإِعْرَاب): Change based on sentence role
  • Definiteness: The noun can be definite (مُعَرَّف) or indefinite (نَكِرَة).

📘 What is I‘rāb (الإِعْرَاب)?

I‘rāb means the grammatical case ending that shows the word's role in a sentence (subject, object, preposition-following). It appears as a vowel at the end of a word.

🔄 3 Main Types of I‘rāb for Nouns:

Case Arabic Ending Function
Raf‘ الرفع ُ (Dammah) Subject / Doer
Nasb النصب َ (Fathah) Object / After certain particles
Jarr الجرّ ِ (Kasrah) After a preposition / Idafah

🧠 Examples of I‘rāb in Action (noun: طالب):

جاءَ طالبٌ. → Talibun (Raf‘ – subject)
رأيتُ طالبًا. → Taliban (Nasb – object)
مررتُ بطالبِ. → Talibin (Jarr – after preposition)

✨ Recognizing Feminine Nouns

While many feminine nouns refer to females, not all do. Arabic has specific signs and conventions to identify feminine nouns:

1. The Taa Marbuta (ـة / ة)

This is the most common sign. If a noun ends with a تَاء مَرْبُوطَة (taa marbuta), it is usually feminine.

  • طالبةٌ (ṭālibatun) - Female student
  • مدينةٌ (madīnatun) - City (even though a city is not biologically female)
  • شجرةٌ (shajaratun) - Tree

2. Alif Maqsoora (ـى)

Nouns ending with ألف مقصورة (a special form of Alif that looks like a `ي` without dots) are feminine.

  • كُبْرَى (kubrā) - Greatest (feminine form)
  • صُغْرَى (ṣughrā) - Smallest (feminine form)

3. Alif Mamdooda (ـاء)

Nouns ending with ألف ممدودة (Alif followed by Hamza) are also typically feminine.

  • حمراء (ḥamrāʼ) - Red (feminine form)
  • صحراء (ṣaḥrāʼ) - Desert

Feminine by Convention (No Obvious Sign):

Some nouns are considered feminine despite not having the above endings. These often include:

  • Paired body parts (e.g., عين - eye, يد - hand)
  • Names of countries and cities (e.g., مصر - Egypt, دمشق - Damascus)
  • Certain natural phenomena (e.g., شمس - sun, نار - fire)
