📌 Arabic Verb Types (أوزان الأفعال)

سالم، معتل، لازم، متعدي — Sound, Weak, Transitive, Intransitive

Arabic verbs are classified by their root structure and grammatical behavior. Understanding these types is key to proper conjugation.

✅ Main Verb Classifications:

Type Arabic Characteristics Example
Sound Verb الفعل السالم No weak letters (و، ي، ا) فَتَحَ (opened)
Weak Verb الفعل المعتل Contains و، ي، ا وَصَلَ (arrived)
Transitive متعدي Takes direct object أَكَلَ (ate)
Intransitive لازم No direct object ذَهَبَ (went)

🎯 Derived Verb Forms (الأوزان):

Verbs are categorized into 10 main forms (I-X) with distinct patterns:

Form Pattern Example Meaning Shift
I فَعَلَ كَتَبَ Basic action
II فَعَّلَ دَرَّسَ Causative
III فاعَلَ قاتَلَ Mutual action
IV أَفْعَلَ أَكْرَمَ Causative

📘 Weak Verb Types:

Type Weak Letter Position Example
Assimilated First radical وَصَلَ (arrived)
Hollow Second radical قَالَ (said)
Defective Third radical دَعَا (invited)

🧠 Form II vs Form IV:

Form II (Intensive): كَسَّرَ (shattered)
vs Basic Form I: كَسَرَ (broke)

Form IV (Causative): أَخْرَجَ (caused to exit)
vs Basic Form I: خَرَجَ (exited)

💡 Pro Tip:

Form II often indicates:
- Intensity (break → shatter)
- Causation (learn → teach)
- Repetition (knock → knock repeatedly)

✍️ Practice:

Identify the verb type:

  1. رَجَعَ → Sound verb
  2. سَاعَدَ → Form III
  3. أَنْزَلَ → Form IV
