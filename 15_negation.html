📌 Negation in Arabic (النفي)

ما، لا، لم، لن — Negative Particles and Structures

Arabic uses specific particles for negation that change based on tense and meaning. Each has distinct grammatical rules.

✅ Main Negative Particles:

Particle Usage Tense Example
ما General negation Past/Present ما فَعَلْتُ (I didn't do)
لا Prohibition/General Present لا تَفْعَلْ (Don't do)
لَمْ Past negation Jussive past لَمْ يَفْعَلْ (He didn't do)
لَنْ Future negation Subjunctive لَنْ أَذْهَبَ (I won't go)

🎯 Negation by Tense:

1. Past Tense Negation

Use ما or لَمْ + jussive verb:

ما كَتَبْتُ الرِّسَالَةَ
I didn't write the letter (neutral tone)
لَمْ أَكْتُبِ الرِّسَالَةَ
I didn't write the letter (emphatic)

2. Present Tense Negation

Use لا or ما + present verb:

لا أَفْهَمُ
I don't understand
ما أُحِبُّ القَهْوَةَ
I don't like coffee (more common in dialects)

3. Future Tense Negation

Use لَنْ + subjunctive verb:

لَنْ أَنْسَى
I will never forget
لَنْ يَنْجَحَ الكَسُولُ
The lazy one won't succeed

📘 Special Negation Cases:

1. Negating Nominal Sentences

Use لَيْسَ (to not be):

لَيْسَ الطَّالِبُ مُجْتَهِدًا
The student is not hardworking

2. Prohibitions

Use لا + jussive:

لا تَدْخُنْ هُنَا
Don't smoke here

🧠 Negation with "Never":

لَمْ أَرَهُ قَطُّ
I never saw him (literally: "I didn't see him ever")

لَنْ أُسَامِحَكَ أَبَدًا
I will never forgive you (literally: "I won't forgive you ever")

📝 Negation Particle Summary:

Particle Verb Form Meaning
لَمْ Jussive (سكون) Did not (past)
لَنْ Subjunctive (فتحة) Will not (future)
لا Present/Jussive Do not (present/prohibition)

✍️ Practice:

Negate these sentences:

  1. ذَهَبَ إِلَى المَدْرَسَةِ → لَمْ يَذْهَبْ إِلَى المَدْرَسَةِ
  2. سَأَزُورُكَ غَدًا → لَنْ أَزُورَكَ غَدًا
  3. هِيَ تَعْرِفُ الجَوَابَ → لَا تَعْرِفُ الجَوَابَ
