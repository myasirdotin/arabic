📌 Arabic Verbs (الأفعال)

الماضي، المضارع، الأمر — Past, Present, Command

Arabic verbs are built on root systems (usually 3 letters) and change form based on tense, person, gender, and number.

✅ The Three Main Tenses:

Tense Arabic Example Meaning
Past ماضي كَتَبَ He wrote
Present مضارع يَكْتُبُ He writes/is writing
Command أمر اُكْتُبْ Write! (masc.)

🎯 Root System (الجذر):

Most verbs derive from 3-letter roots:

ك - ت - ب

This root gives us words related to "writing":

  • كَتَبَ (he wrote)
  • كِتابٌ (book)
  • مَكْتَبَةٌ (library)
  • كَاتِبٌ (writer)
  • مَكْتُوبٌ (written/letter)

📘 Past Tense Conjugation:

Person Arabic Example (كَتَبَ)
I تُ كَتَبْتُ
You (masc.) تَ كَتَبْتَ
You (fem.) تِ كَتَبْتِ
He َ كَتَبَ
She َتْ كَتَبَتْ
We نَا كَتَبْنَا
They (masc.) وا كَتَبُوا
They (fem.) نَ كَتَبْنَ

📝 Present Tense Prefixes/Suffixes:

Person Prefix Example (يَكْتُبُ)
I أ أَكْتُبُ
You (masc.) ت تَكْتُبُ
You (fem.) ت تَكْتُبِينَ
He ي يَكْتُبُ
She ت تَكْتُبُ
We ن نَكْتُبُ
They (masc.) ي يَكْتُبُونَ
They (fem.) ي يَكْتُبْنَ

🔍 Verb Patterns (أوزان):

Arabic verbs follow different patterns that change meaning:

  • فَعَلَ (Form I) - Basic meaning: كَتَبَ (to write)
  • فَعَّلَ (Form II) - Intensive: دَرَّسَ (to teach)
  • فَاعَلَ (Form III) - Mutual action: جَاهَدَ (to struggle)
  • أَفْعَلَ (Form IV) - Causative: أَكْرَمَ (to honor)

🧠 Verb Sentence Examples:

كَتَبَ الرِّسالَةَ (He wrote the letter)

تَكْتُبُ الواجِبَ (You [fem.] are writing the homework)

اِقْرَأِ الكِتابَ (Read the book! [masc.])

سَنَذْهَبُ غَدًا (We will go tomorrow)

لَمْ يَأْكُلْ (He did not eat)

⚠️ Important Notes:

  1. Present tense often implies continuous action ("is writing")
  2. Command forms change based on gender/number
  3. Verbs agree with subjects in gender/number
  4. Negation uses special particles: لَمْ (past), لا (present)
  5. Future is formed by adding سَ or سَوْفَ to present tense

✍️ Practice:

Conjugate these verbs:

  1. He ate (أَكَلَ) → أَكَلَ
  2. I study (يَدْرُسُ) → أَدْرُسُ
  3. You (masc.) write! → اُكْتُبْ
  4. They (fem.) read → يَقْرَأْنَ
  5. We went → ذَهَبْنَا
