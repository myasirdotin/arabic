📌 Verb Doubling in Arabic (التضعيف)

الفعل المضعف — Form II, IV and Beyond

Arabic verb doubling (تضعيف) refers to verbs where the second and third root letters are identical, creating intensive or causative meanings.

✅ Key Doubled Verb Forms:

Form Pattern Example Meaning Shift
Form I فَعَلَ مَدَّ Basic action (to extend)
Form II فَعَّلَ مَدَّدَ Intensive/causative (to prolong)
Form IV أَفْعَلَ أَمَدَّ Causative (to cause to extend)

🎯 Form II Doubling Rules:

1. Structure

Second radical is doubled with shaddah:

فَـعَّـلَ

2. Meaning Changes

  • Intensification: قَطَعَ (cut) → قَطَّعَ (cut into pieces)
  • Causation: عَلِمَ (know) → عَلَّمَ (teach)
  • Repetition: دَقَّ (knocked repeatedly)

3. Conjugation Example (مَدَّدَ)

يُمَدِّدُ (he prolongs, present)
مَدَّدُوا (they prolonged, past)

📘 Form IV Doubling Rules:

1. Structure

Hamza prefix + doubled second radical:

أَفْـعَّـلَ

2. Meaning Changes

  • Strong causation: أَكْرَمَ (honored someone)
  • Transformative: أَحَبَّ (made someone love)

3. Conjugation Example (أَمَدَّ)

يُمِدُّ (he causes to extend, present)
أَمَدُّوا (they caused to extend, past)

📝 Doubled Verb Types:

Type Root Example Challenge
Regular م-د-د مَدَّ Normal conjugation
Assimilated و-د-د وَدَّ Initial weak letter
Hollow ق-و-ل قَالَ Middle weak letter

🧠 Pronunciation Tips:

Shaddah Emphasis: Hold the doubled consonant twice as long

مَدَّ = mad-da (not ma-da)

يُمَدِّدُ = yu-ma-ddi-du (4 syllables)

✍️ Practice:

Convert these Form I verbs to Form II:

  1. كَسَرَ (break) → كَسَّرَ (shatter)
  2. ضَرَبَ (hit) → ضَرَّبَ (hit repeatedly)
  3. فَهِمَ (understand) → فَهَّمَ (explain)
