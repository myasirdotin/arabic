📌 Possession in Arabic (الإضافة)

المضاف والمضاف إليه — The Construct Phrase

The Idaafa (إضافة) is Arabic's unique way to show possession or relationship between two nouns, like "the teacher's book" or "the door of the house".

✅ Basic Idaafa Structure:

مضاف + مضاف إليه
(Owned) + (Owner)
كِتابُ المُعَلِّمِ
The teacher's book (Literally: "Book of the teacher")

🎯 Key Rules:

Rule Example Explanation
1. No "الـ" on first noun بابُ البيتِ (not البابُ) First noun becomes automatically definite
2. Second noun takes genitive case (ـِ) كِتابُ الطالِبِ Always ends with kasrah
3. Adjective describes first noun كِتابُ الطالِبِ الجَديدُ "The student's new book"

📘 Types of Idaafa:

1. Possessive

Ownership relationships:

سَيَّارَةُ الأُسْتاذِ
The professor's car

2. Descriptive

Characteristic/Type:

بابُ الخَشَبِ
The wooden door (door of wood)

🧠 Special Cases:

1. Pronouns in Idaafa

Attached pronouns act as owners:

كِتابُهُ (his book)
بَيْتُها (her house)

2. Multiple Owners

Chain Idaafa phrases:

كِتابُ طالِبِ العِلْمِ
The student of knowledge's book

📝 Idaafa vs. English Possessive:

Arabic English Structure
قَلَمُ الوَلَدِ The boy's pen Pen + of + the boy
شُبَّاكُ الغُرْفَةِ The room's window Window + of + the room

💡 Pro Tip:

The first noun loses tanween and becomes definite even without "الـ":

كِتابٌ (a book) → كِتابُ المُعَلِّمِ (the teacher's book)

  2. My friend's house → بَيْتُ صَديقي
