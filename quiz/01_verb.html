📌 Arabic Verbs (الأفعال)

الماضي، المضارع، الأمر — Past, Present, Command

Arabic verbs are built on root systems (usually 3 letters) and change form based on tense, person, gender, and number.

✅ The Three Main Tenses:

Tense Arabic Example Meaning
Past ماضي كَتَبَ He wrote
Present مضارع يَكْتُبُ He writes/is writing
Command أمر اُكْتُبْ Write! (masc.)

🎯 Root System (الجذر):

Most verbs derive from 3-letter roots:

ك - ت - ب

This root gives us words related to "writing":

  • كَتَبَ (he wrote)
  • كِتابٌ (book)
  • مَكْتَبَةٌ (library)

📘 Past Tense Conjugation:

Person Arabic Example (كَتَبَ)
I تُ كَتَبْتُ
You (masc.) تَ كَتَبْتَ
You (fem.) تِ كَتَبْتِ
He َ كَتَبَ
She َتْ كَتَبَتْ

📝 Present Tense Prefixes/Suffixes:

Person Prefix Example (يَكْتُبُ)
I أ أَكْتُبُ
You (masc.) ت تَكْتُبُ
You (fem.) ت تَكْتُبِينَ
He ي يَكْتُبُ
She ت تَكْتُبُ

🧠 Verb Sentence Examples:

كَتَبَ الرِّسالَةَ (He wrote the letter)

تَكْتُبُ الواجِبَ (You [fem.] are writing the homework)

اِقْرَأِ الكِتابَ (Read the book! [masc.])

⚠️ Important Notes:

  1. Present tense often implies continuous action ("is writing")
  2. Command forms change based on gender/number
  3. Verbs agree with subjects in gender/number
