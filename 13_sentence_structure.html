📌 Arabic Sentence Structure (تركيب الجملة)

الجملة الاسمية والجملة الفعلية — Nominal vs. Verbal Sentences

Arabic sentences follow two main patterns: nominal (starts with noun) and verbal (starts with verb). Each has distinct rules.

✅ The Two Sentence Types:

Type Arabic Structure Example
Nominal جملة اسمية Subject + Predicate الكِتابُ جَديدٌ
Verbal جملة فعلية Verb + Subject + Object قَرَأَ الوَلَدُ الكِتابَ

🎯 Nominal Sentence (الجملة الاسمية):

مبتدأ + خبر
(Subject) + (Predicate)

Key Rules:

  1. Subject (مبتدأ) is always definite
  2. Predicate (خبر) is usually indefinite
  3. Both take nominative case (رفع) by default
البَيْتُ كَبِيرٌ
The house is big
الطالِبُ مُجْتَهِدٌ
The student is hardworking

📘 Verbal Sentence (الجملة الفعلية):

فعل + فاعل + مفعول
(Verb) + (Subject) + (Object)

Key Rules:

  1. Verb agrees with subject in gender/number
  2. Subject is nominative (رفع)
  3. Object is accusative (نصب)
شَرِبَتِ البِنْتُ الحَلِيبَ
The girl drank the milk
سَيَكْتُبُ الأُسْتَاذُ الدَّرْسَ
The teacher will write the lesson

📝 Sentence Components:

Component Arabic Role Case
Subject الفاعل Doer of action رفع
Object المفعول به Receiver of action نصب
Predicate الخبر Describes subject رفع

🧠 Advanced Structures:

1. Inverted Sentences

Verb comes first for emphasis:

فِي البَيْتِ وَلَدٌ
In the house is a boy

2. Kaana Sentences

Using "كان" and sisters:

كانَ الكِتابُ جَديداً
The book was new

✍️ Practice:

Identify sentence type:

  1. المُعَلِّمُ حاضِرٌ → Nominal
  2. يَبْنِي العامِلُ البَيْتَ → Verbal
  3. أَصْبَحَ الجَوُّ بَارِداً → Kaana nominal
