📌 Adjectives in Arabic (الصفات)

النعت والمنعوت — Describing Nouns

Adjectives in Arabic must agree with the nouns they describe in four key aspects: gender, number, case, and definiteness.

✅ The 4 Agreements:

Feature Noun Example Adjective Form
Gender كِتابٌ (masc.) جَديدٌ (new)
سَيَّارَةٌ (fem.) جَديدَةٌ
Number طالِبٌ (sing.) مُجْتَهِدٌ (hardworking)
طالِباتٌ (pl.) مُجْتَهِداتٌ
Case البابُ (raf') الكبيرُ (big)
رأيتُ البابَ (nasb) الكبيرَ
Definiteness كِتابٌ (indef.) جَديدٌ
الكِتابُ (def.) الجَديدُ

🎯 Adjective Placement:

1. After the Noun

Most adjectives come after the noun:

بَيْتٌ كَبيرٌ
A big house

2. Exceptions

Some adjectives come before:

كُلُّ طالِبٍ
Every student (كُلُّ = "every")

📘 Common Adjective Patterns:

فَعيل (Fa'eel)

For permanent qualities:

جَميلٌ (beautiful), طَويلٌ (tall)

أَفْعَل (Af'al)

For colors/defects:

أَحْمَرُ (red), أَعْوَرُ (one-eyed)

Feminine form: حَمْراءُ

🧠 Agreement in Sentences:

Masculine: الوَلَدُ الصَّغيرُ يَلْعَبُ
The small boy plays

Feminine: البِنْتُ الصَّغيرَةُ تَلْعَبُ
The small girl plays

Plural: الأَوْلادُ الصِّغارُ يَلْعَبونَ
The small children play

📝 Adjective Types:

Type Example Notes
Descriptive جَميلٌ (beautiful) Most common type
Color أَبْيَضُ (white) Follow أَفْعَل pattern
Demonstrative هذا (this) Technically nouns but function as adjectives
