📌 Gender in Arabic Nouns (المذكر والمؤنث)

الجنس في الأسماء — Masculine & Feminine

Arabic nouns are either masculine (مذكر) or feminine (مؤنث). Unlike English, grammatical gender affects adjectives, verbs, and pronouns.

✅ Rules for Feminine Nouns:

Most feminine nouns end with one of these markers:

  • ـة (Tā’ Marbūṭah): مدرسة (school), سيارة (car)
  • ـاء / ـى: سماء (sky), عليا (upper)
  • Exceptions: Some feminine nouns lack markers (e.g., أم "mother", شمس "sun").

🎯 Examples:

Arabic English Gender Marker
كِتاب Book Masculine —
طالبة Student (f) Feminine ـة
مُعَلِّمة Teacher (f) Feminine ـة
حَرْباء Chameleon Feminine ـاء

📘 How to Identify Gender:

Tip 1: Default Gender

Nouns without feminine markers are usually masculine (e.g., باب "door").

Tip 2: Meaning-Based

Nouns for female beings are feminine even without markers: مريم (Maryam), ناقة (female camel).

🧠 Practice Examples:

الولدُ (the boy) → Masculine
البنْتُ (the girl) → Feminine (ـة)
قلمٌ (a pen) → Masculine
ورقةٌ (a paper) → Feminine (ـة)

📝 Summary:

Term Arabic Key Point
Masculine مذكر No marker (usually)
Feminine مؤنث Often ends with ـة, ـاء, ـى
