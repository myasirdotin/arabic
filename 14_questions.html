📌 Question Formation in Arabic (أسئلة)

أداة الاستفهام + الجملة — Interrogatives & Question Structures

Arabic questions are formed using specific question words and particles that appear at the beginning of sentences. The word order typically remains unchanged.

✅ Main Question Words:

Question Arabic Usage Example
What? مَا / مَاذَا Things/actions مَا هَذَا؟ (What is this?)
Who? مَنْ People مَنْ فَتَحَ البَابَ؟ (Who opened the door?)
Where? أَيْنَ Location أَيْنَ الكِتَابُ؟ (Where is the book?)
When? مَتَى Time مَتَى سَتَذْهَبُ؟ (When will you go?)
Why? لِمَاذَا Reason لِمَاذَا بَكَيْتَ؟ (Why did you cry?)
How? كَيْفَ Manner كَيْفَ حَالُكَ؟ (How are you?)
Which? أَيُّ Choice أَيُّ كِتَابٍ تُحِبُّ؟ (Which book do you like?)

🎯 Yes/No Questions:

Using "Hal" (هَلْ) or "A" (أَ)

These particles convert statements to yes/no questions without changing word order:

Statement: الطَّالِبُ ذَكِيٌّ (The student is smart)

Question: هَلِ الطَّالِبُ ذَكِيٌّ؟ (Is the student smart?)

Alternative: أَذَكِيٌّ الطَّالِبُ؟ (Is the student smart?)

هَلْ is more common in Modern Standard Arabic, while أَ appears more in Classical Arabic.

📘 Question Word Placement:

1. With Nominal Sentences

Question word + nominal sentence:

مَا هَذِهِ؟
What is this? (feminine)

2. With Verbal Sentences

Question word + verbal sentence:

كَيْفَ ذَهَبْتَ إِلَى المَدْرَسَةِ؟
How did you go to school?

🧠 Special Cases:

1. "How much/many?"

Use كَمْ followed by a singular noun in accusative case:

كَمْ كِتَاباً عِنْدَكَ؟
How many books do you have? (Literally: "How much book...")

2. "Why?" Alternatives

لِمَ (short for لِمَاذَا) is more conversational:

لِمَ تَضْحَكُ؟
Why are you laughing?

📝 Summary Table:

Question Type Arabic Tool Example
Information ماذا، مَنْ، أَيْنَ مَاذَا تَقْرَأُ؟
Yes/No هَلْ / أَ هَلْ تَفْهَمُ؟
