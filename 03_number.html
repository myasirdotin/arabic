📌 Number in Arabic Nouns (العدد: مفرد، مثنى، جمع)

العدد في الأسماء — Singular, Dual, and Plural

Arabic nouns change form based on number: singular (one), dual (two), or plural (three+). Unlike English, Arabic has a dedicated dual form!

✅ The Three Numbers:

Number Arabic Ending Example
Singular مفرد — كتابٌ (a book)
Dual مثنى ـانِ / ـَيْنِ كتابانِ (two books)
Plural جمع Varies كُتُبٌ (books)

🎯 Dual Form Rules:

  • Nominative (رفع): Add ـانِ (-āni)
    طالب → طالبانِ (two students)
  • Accusative/Genitive (نصب/جر): Add ـَيْنِ (-ayni)
    رأيتُ طالبَيْنِ (I saw two students)

📘 Plural Types:

1. Sound Masculine Plural (جمع مذكر سالم)

Add ـونَ (nominative) or ـينَ (accusative/genitive):
مُعَلِّم → مُعَلِّمونَ (male teachers)

2. Sound Feminine Plural (جمع مؤنث سالم)

Add ـاتٌ:
مُعَلِّمة → مُعَلِّماتٌ (female teachers)

3. Broken Plural (جمع التكسير)

Irregular pattern – must be memorized:
كِتاب → كُتُبٌ (books), بَيت → بُيوتٌ (houses)

🧠 Practice Examples:

Singular Dual Plural
قلمٌ (pen) قلمانِ أقلامٌ (sound)
مدرسةٌ (school) مدرستانِ مدارسُ (broken)
