📌 Demonstratives in Arabic (أسماء الإشارة)

هذا، هذه، أولئك — Pointing Words

Arabic demonstratives change based on gender, number, and distance. They function as both pronouns ("this is...") and adjectives ("this book").

✅ Basic Demonstratives:

English Arabic Used For Example
This (masc.) هَذا Masculine singular هَذا كِتابٌ
This (fem.) هَذِهِ Feminine singular هَذِهِ سَيَّارَةٌ
These (dual) هَذانِ / هَاتانِ Two items هَذانِ وَلَدانِ
Those (pl.) أُولٰئِكَ Far plural أُولٰئِكَ رِجالٌ

🎯 Rules for Use:

1. As Pronouns

Stand alone to point at things:

هَذا مُحَمَّدٌ
This is Muhammad

2. As Adjectives

Describe a following noun:

هَذِهِ الطالِبَةُ ذَكِيَّةٌ
This student (fem.) is smart

📘 Distance-Based Forms:

Near (This/These)

  • هَذا (masc.)
  • هَذِهِ (fem.)
  • هٰؤُلاءِ (pl.)

Far (That/Those)

  • ذٰلِكَ (masc.)
  • تِلْكَ (fem.)
  • أُولٰئِكَ (pl.)

🧠 Dual Forms:

Masculine Dual:
هَذانِ الوَلَدانِ (These two boys)

Feminine Dual:
هَاتانِ البِنْتانِ (These two girls)

Note: Endings change to ـَانِ in nominative, ـَيْنِ in accusative/genitive.

📝 Summary Table:

Distance Masculine Feminine Plural
Near هَذا هَذِهِ هٰؤُلاءِ
Far ذٰلِكَ تِلْكَ أُولٰئِكَ

⚠️ Important Note:

أُولٰئِكَ is used only for people. For objects, repeat the singular:
تِلْكَ الكُتُبُ (Those books)

✍️ Practice:

Translate these demonstratives:

  1. That teacher (fem.) → تِلْكَ المُعَلِّمَةُ
  2. These two houses → هَاتانِ البَيْتَانِ
  3. Those men → أُولٰئِكَ الرِّجالُ
